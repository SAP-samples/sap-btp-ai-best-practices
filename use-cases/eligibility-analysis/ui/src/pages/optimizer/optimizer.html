Deferral Drivers by Planning Week

Combined bottleneck headroom (€) is the minimum remaining headroom across all active facility, customer, and group limits for each week.

Formula per week: min(limit - used_total) over all entities with a configured limit.

It is not a sum. Lower values mean the plan is close to at least one credit constraint.

The utilization lines show the maximum utilization reached by facility, customer, and group limits in that same week.

Stacked bars show deferred invoice counts by driver (facility/customer/group/other) per planning week.

When a deferred row references multiple drivers in its detail, it is split proportionally across those drivers.

Other means no explicit facility/customer/group driver was detected in the exclusion detail or reason text (fallback bucket).

The green line shows total deferred amount in euro for each week.

Shows how scheduled invoice exposure builds up and decays over the planning horizon.

Cumulative outstanding exposure (red): total value of all invoices that have been scheduled and have not yet matured. Once an invoice is scheduled it remains outstanding until its expected lifetime expires.

Active invoices in lifetime (green): value of invoices whose processing window includes that week -- from their scheduled date until their lifetime ends.

When the green line drops below the red, earlier-scheduled invoices are still outstanding even though fewer invoices are actively being processed. The gap represents maturing exposure: money that is committed but winding down.

Credit Limits

Automatic: Limits are calculated from the current cohort candidates after rules are applied (before optimization).

  • Facility limit: total candidate amount per company code, multiplied by the facility percentage.
  • Customer limit: company facility limit, multiplied by the customer percentage.
  • Group limit: mapped company facility limit, multiplied by the group percentage (only when group mapping exists).

Example: If Company Code 2 has 99,861.30 candidate amount, a facility percentage of 0.85 gives 84,882.10. Then a customer percentage of 0.15 gives 12,732.32 as the default customer limit in that company.

Manual: You provide exact limits directly; explicit values override automatic defaults.
Automatic Manual
Facility credit ceiling:
Sets the facility percentage used in automatic mode. For each company code, the system takes the total candidate amount in the selected cohort and applies this percentage to derive the facility limit. Lower values create tighter company-level capacity.
Customer concentration limit:
Sets the customer concentration percentage used when a customer does not have an explicit limit. The default customer limit is calculated as this percentage of that customer's company facility limit. If an invoice is larger than the resulting customer limit, it cannot be selected.
Group concentration limit:
Sets the group concentration percentage used for customer groups when group mapping is available. The default group limit is calculated from the mapped company facility limit. If no group mapping is provided, this constraint is inactive.
Manual Limits File (YAML or Excel):
Upload Limits File
Upload a YAML/JSON file with manual limits payload, or the standard limits Excel template (for example data/2026/new set with limits/Limit_template BTP.xlsx).
Facility Limits (JSON):
Enter exact credit limits for each company code. You can paste full JSON or only the object content (no outer braces). Example: "1000": 15000000 sets a 15M limit for company code 1000.
Customer Limits (JSON):
Enter exact credit limits for specific customers. Full JSON and brace-free object content are both accepted. Customers not listed here use the default calculated from the facility limit.
Group Limits (JSON):
Enter exact credit limits for related customer groups. You can provide full JSON or only key/value entries without outer braces.
Customer to Group Mapping (JSON):
Map each customer to a group ID. This mapping is required for group limits and group base exposure to take effect.
Base Exposure
Facility Base Exposure (JSON):
Pre-used exposure at planning start per company code. This value reduces available facility headroom before selecting new invoices.
Customer Base Exposure (JSON):
Pre-used exposure per customer at planning start. If this is close to the customer limit, only small additional invoices can be selected.
Group Base Exposure (JSON):
Pre-used exposure per group at planning start. Group usage is applied only when customer-to-group mapping exists.
