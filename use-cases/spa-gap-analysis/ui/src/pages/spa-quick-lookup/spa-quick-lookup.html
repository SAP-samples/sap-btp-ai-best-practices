COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)

Definition: The total amount spent by the customer on purchased materials and products.

Data Period: Last 12 months (rolling quarterly data)
Source: Quarterly sales transactions aggregated from Q1-Q4
Updated: Quarterly (most recent data: 2026-04-13)

RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)

Definition: Customer segmentation model based on purchase behavior:

  • Recency: How recently the customer made a purchase
  • Frequency: How often they purchase
  • Monetary: How much they spend

Segments: Champions, Loyal, Promising, At Risk, Need Attention, Lost
Updated: Quarterly based on rolling 12-month transaction history

Savings Analysis (Current Savings on Existing SPAs)

Definition: CURRENT cost savings already achieved through existing SPA (Special Price Agreement) pricing.

⚠️ Important: This shows actual savings on active SPAs, NOT potential savings from missing SPAs.

How Savings are Calculated:
  1. Filter materials with active SPAs: Only materials with both base cost data AND active SPA pricing
  2. Calculate difference: Savings = Base Cost - SPA Price (for each material)

📊 Two Ways to View Savings %:
Savings % can be viewed as average discount on SPA materials, or as % of total spending

Coverage: Savings calculated only for materials with SPA pricing data
Source: A703 SPA price nets, base cost data, quarterly sales transactions
Updated: Real-time based on active SPA agreements

SPA (Special Price Agreement)

Definition: Pre-negotiated pricing contracts with suppliers for specific materials or material groups.

Types: Blanket SPAs (broad material coverage) and Customer-Specific SPAs
Updated: Real-time from SAP master data
